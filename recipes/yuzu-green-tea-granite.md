# Yuzu–Green Tea Granité

**Silken tofu cream · white sesame tuile**

---

- **Style:** Cold, aromatic, restrained
- **Role:** Light dessert after a rich meal
- **Yield:** 4 desserts

---

## Component 1 — Yuzu–Green Tea Granité

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Water | 360 ml | |
| Sencha green tea | 12 g | Loose leaf |
| Sugar | 90 g | |
| Yuzu juice | 85 ml | Or substitute below |
| Fine salt | 0.8 g | |

**Yuzu substitute:** 40 ml lemon juice + 35 ml mandarin juice + 3 ml rice vinegar

### Method (T – 24 h)

1. Heat water to 70°C
2. Steep tea for 90 seconds
3. Strain immediately through fine sieve
4. Dissolve sugar while liquid is still warm
5. Add citrus juice and salt
6. Chill base completely (2–4 hours)
7. Pour into shallow metal tray (≤1.5 cm depth)
8. Freeze uncovered
9. Scrape with fork every 45 minutes until dry, fine crystals form
10. Cover loosely and hold frozen

**Target:** Dry, fluffy crystals — no slush

**Yield:** ~500 ml granité

---

## Component 2 — Silken Tofu Cream

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Silken tofu | 150 g | Drained, patted dry |
| Icing sugar | 15–20 g | Adjust to taste |
| White tahini | 15 g | Adds body, echoes tuile |
| White miso | ½ tsp | Depth without identifiable flavour |
| Yuzu zest | Scant ¼ tsp | |
| Fine salt | Pinch | |

### Method (T – 4 h or earlier)

1. Blend all ingredients until completely smooth
2. Pass through fine sieve for true silk texture
3. Transfer to container and rest 20–30 minutes (allows air bubbles to settle)
4. Taste and adjust sweetness/salt
5. Refrigerate until service

**Note:** Can weep if plated too far ahead. Spoon just before adding granité.

**Yield:** ~180 g cream

---

## Component 3 — White Sesame Tuile

### Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Egg white | 25 g | ~1 small egg white |
| Sugar | 30 g | |
| Unsalted butter | 15 g | Melted, warm |
| White sesame seeds | 20 g | |
| Plain flour | 8 g | |
| Fine salt | Pinch | |

### Method (T – 1 d or earlier)

1. Whisk egg white and sugar just to combine (no foam)
2. Whisk in melted butter
3. Add flour and salt, whisk smooth
4. Fold in sesame seeds
5. Rest batter 15–20 minutes
6. Spread paper-thin (~1 mm) on r silicone mat — roughly A4 size per tuile
7. Bake at 170°C (fan off) for 15-25 minutes till deep golden syrup colour, rotating a few times to brown evenly
8. For shards: cool flat, then break
9. For curves: drape warm tuile over rolling pin or press into ring mould

**Yield:** 6–8 tuiles

**Storage:** Airtight container at room temperature. One shard per plate.

---

## Plating

### Prep

- Freeze shallow serving bowls
- Scrape granité to refresh crystals shortly before service

### Assembly (per plate)

1. Spoon 1 Tbsp silken tofu cream off-centre
2. Add 120–140 ml granité, loosely mounded
3. Lean one sesame tuile shard into the ice
4. Express yuzu peel oil over the surface (lemon as fallback)
5. Finish with light microplaned yuzu zest
6. Optional: one shiso microleaf

**Do not compress the granité. Negative space is intentional.**

## Notes

- **Tea steeping:** 90 seconds at 70°C prevents bitterness. Taste at 60 seconds if using a new batch — some sencha turns tannic quickly.
- **Tahini and miso:** Both are optional but recommended. The tahini adds body and creates a flavour bridge to the sesame tuile. The miso provides quiet depth without identifiable flavour. Either can be reduced or omitted if the cream tastes too busy.
- **Granité texture:** The 17% sugar ratio keeps crystals dry and scrapable. For a more austere version, reduce sugar to 80 g.
- **Tofu cream stability:** Unlike dairy, silken tofu can weep. Plate the cream immediately before adding granité.
- **Tuile shaping:** Work quickly while warm. Once cool, tuiles become brittle and will crack if bent.
